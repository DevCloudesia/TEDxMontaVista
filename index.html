Prepare
Speaker resources

Whether it's your first talk or your fifth, these will help you craft something memorable.

01

Finding Your Idea

Every great talk starts with one idea you genuinely care about.

  • Pick ONE thing — not three, not five
  • Make it personal. Why does this keep you up at night?
  • Ask yourself: what should the audience think differently after?
  • Run it by a friend. If they lean in, you're onto something
02

Writing It

Structure is what makes the difference between rambling and riveting.

  • Open with a hook — a question, a moment, a surprise
  • Follow the arc: What → So What → Now What
  • Shoot for 8–12 minutes (~1,200–1,800 words)
  • End on something your audience will actually remember
03

Delivering It

You've written something great. Now make people feel it.

  • Rehearse out loud at least 10 times (yes, really)
  • Record yourself. It's painful but it works
  • Pauses > filler words. Silence is your friend
  • Move with intention, not because you're nervous
04

Designing Slides

Your slides should support what you're saying, not repeat it.

  • One idea per slide — that's it
  • Use images instead of bullet points
  • Under 10 words per slide
  • Dark backgrounds, minimal transitions
